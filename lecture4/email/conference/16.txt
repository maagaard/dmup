           THE COPENHAGEN SYMPOSIUM ON VISION AND VISUAL COGNITION

          An international symposium on Vision and Visual Cognition is
          held 25-27 April 1997 at the Southern Campus (KUA) of the
          University of Copenhagen (Njalsgade 80, lecture hall 10.1.30A).
          The symposium is organized by Claus Bundesen and Axel Larsen.
          The main topics of the symposium are (1) Basic Sensory
          Processes, (2) Recognition and Categorization, (3) Selective
          Attention, and (4) Mental Imagery. The emphasis is on
          computational and neuropsychological perspectives. The programme
          is as follows.

          --------------------------------------------------------------------

          ---------------------------------------------------------------------

          13.00 Welcome

                  BASIC SENSORY PROCESSES

          13.05 Roger J. Watt, University of Stirling, Scotland, UK  What
          clues to visual processing can we find in the visual
          environment?

          psychophysics of perceptual memory

          14.50 Coffee break
