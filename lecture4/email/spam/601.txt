ACM, the Association for Computing Machinery, is proud
to announce an important new publication, "Intellectual Property
in the Age of Universal Access."

Learn more about it now at  http://www.acm.org/pubs/property/

"Intellectual Property in the Age of Universal Access" is
written by some of the most influential figures in intellectual
property law and information technology, including Pamela
Samuelson and Peter Neumann. This compendium of seventeen
articles is sure to prove an invaluable resource for lawyers, law
students, IT specialists, and business managers.

For more information or to order, please visit our
website at  http://www.acm.org/pubs/property/

"Intellectual Property in the Age of Universal Access" includes
 topics such as:

* Trademark disputes
* E-commerce laws and long-arm jurisdictions
* The Communications Decency Act
* Practical legalities of software reverse engineering
* Encoding the law of digital libraries
* Privacy considerations
* Look-and-feel and fair use lawsuits
* Cryptography's role in securing information
* Does information really need to be licensed?
* Embedding technical self-help in licensed software
* Digital signatures, digital cash
* Digital Millenium Copyright Act
* Database protection

To understand intellectual property, you must first
understand the technology that fuels it.
