The French Atomic Energy Commission (CEA) invites applications for two
post-doctoral positions in the Image Analysis and Data Analysis groups 
of the Laboratory of Anatomical and Functional Neuroimaging: 

1 Postdoctoral position in brain image analysis
1 Postdoctoral position in brain statistical image analysis

For all these positions, a first deadline is May 20, 1999, nevertheless latter
candidatures will also be considered.

Fellowships include full salary support plus research and travel expenses up
to two years. MRI facilities include a Bruker 3 Tesla EPI capable scanner
and a General Electric 1.5 Tesla scanner capable of EPI and equipped with a
CRM (40mT/m) coil. The SHFJ also houses 2 PET cameras, a cyclotron and an
extensive computational infrastructure. The successful candidate will join a
multidisciplinary team of computer scientists, MR physicists, cognitive
neuroscientists, neurologists... Orsay is located 35 min from central Paris.


Postdoctoral position in brain image analysis:
----------------------------------------------
Investigation of the living human brain structural connectivity
from MR diffusion imaging

The project relies on diffusion tensor MRI which allows white matter fascicle
orientation to be mapped. An image illustrating the tracking of a
cortico-spinal fascicle using MR diffusion tensor is attached to this mail.
More information on this subject can be found in: C. Poupon et al.,
Regularization of MR diffusion tensor maps for
tracking brain white matter bundles, MICCAI, MIT, oct. 98, LNCS-1496,
Springer Verlag, pp 489-498. It is expected that fellows have a strong
background in image analysis (MRF, pattern recognition, graph theory...) 
and computer science (C++, OpenGL, patterns, Linux...). Knowledge in 
neurosciences would be appreciated.

Send CV, statement of research, background and interests to J-F Mangin
(mangin@shfj.cea.fr).


Postdoctoral position in brain statistical image analysis:
----------------------------------------------------------
One postdoctoral position (2 years) is available in SHFJ data analysis
group to participate at the development of methods for statistical 
functional brain images analyses. The group has ongoing collaboration 
with the F.I.L. and the McGill university (K.Friston and K.Worsley). 
The candidate should have background in statistics and computer science
(Unix/Linux, matlab, C/C++). Knowledge in neurosciences (particularly 
brain mapping) would be appreciated.

Send CV, statement of research, background and interests to J-B. Poline
(poline@shfj.cea.fr).
