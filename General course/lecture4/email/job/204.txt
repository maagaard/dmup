			University of Florida
			Assistant Professor

The Department of Clinical and Health Psychology and the NIMH Center for
the Study of Emotion and Attention are interested in hiring a tenure-line
Assistant Professor whose research and teaching interests are in cognition
and emotion and their applications for the psychopathology of anxiety and
depression.  The basic duties of the successful candidate will include:
(a) conducting psychophysiological and functional neuroimaging research
into emotion and affective disorders at the Center for the Study of
Emotion and Attention, (b) clinical and research supervision of graduate
students and interns in the Departments fear and anxiety clinic, and (c)
teaching in the areas of clinical-cognitive neuroscience, experimental
design and analysis, or experimental psychopathology.  The individual will
be housed at the University of Florida laboratory of the NIMH Center for
the Study of Emotion and Attention, and will carry out their clinical and
teaching duties in the Department of Clinical and Health Psychology.  The
facilities of the Center, including laboratory space, instrumentation,
laboratory assistants, and bio-engineering and secretarial support will be
available to the appointee.  It is planned that the successful candidate
will devote full time to research and research related activities in the
first year of the appointment, and will receive half-time Center research
support for up to three subsequent years. During this initial period, it
is anticipated that the appointee will develop their own grant-supported
research program, consonant with the overall aims of the Center and
Department.  Appropriate qualifications for consideration include:  (a) A
Ph.D. in clinical psychology and an internship from APA-accredited
institutions; (b) research interests and experience in anxiety disorders,
cognitive neuroscience, and experimental psychopathology; (c) technical,
psychophysiological laboratory and computer programming skills, including
experience in the acquisition, reduction, and analysis of high-density
event-related potentials and/or functional magnetic resonance imaging
data; (d) license eligibility as a psychologist in Florida, and (e)
evidence that the applicant can obtain external grant funding.
Interested candidates should submit a cover letter, curriculum vitae and
the names and addresses of three references to:  Peter J. Lang, Ph.D., Box
100165, Gainesville , FL 32610-0165; Phone (352) 392-2439; FAX (352)
392-6047.  EMAIL: langlab@nersp.nerdc.ufl.edu.  Application materials are
due by February 1, 2000.  EEO/AAE Employee searches are conducted under
the provision of Florida Sunshine Law.
