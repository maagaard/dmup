 To Whom It May Concern:
 
 The Laboratory for Affective Neuroscience at the University of Wisconsin, 
 led by Dr. Richard J. Davidson, is currently looking for a full-time 
 research specialist:
 
 ****MINIMUM NUMBER OF YEARS AND TYPE OF RELEVANT WORK EXPERIENCE*****
  At least 1 year's experience with psychological and/or biological research
  in a laboratory setting.  Knowledge of neuroanatomy necessary, as well as 
  knowledge of IBM compatible, UNIX, and/or VAX computer systems.
  
 ****PRINCIPAL DUTIES*****
  Conduct multi-session neuroimaging studies in depressed and normal adult
  populations, coordinating with technical and clinical staff as well as 
  students.  Conduct experimental sessions in which functional magnetic 
  resonance imaging (fMRI), positron emission tomography (FDG-PET), and 
  electroencephalography (EEG) data are obtained.  Includes subject 
  recruitment & scheduling, application of sensors, working with blood, and
  supervision of student help.  Perform data reduction, coregistration of
  structural MRI to fMRI and PET scans, and anatomical localization on 
  structural MRI data.  Also includes data bases maintenance and statistical
  analyses of fMRI, PET, and EEG data. Knowledge of SPM and brain anatomy
  helpful.
