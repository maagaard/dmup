Dear All:

As you may be aware, the University of Arizona Center for Consciousness 
Studies is holding a workshop at LO-Skolen from the 21-24 of August, 
1997.  This workshop is being planned in conjunction with a Symposium on 
Neuropphilosopphy and Ethics to be held in Copenhagen from the 18 - 20 of 
August.  The Symposium is a HjerneAaret event and open to the general 
public.  The workshop is a small meeting (approx. 250 participants) of 
researchers and students of brain science and consciousness studies.  

THe main topics to be addressed at the workshop are: The neural basis of 
consciousness, The Self, and Bridging the Explanatory Gap.  The confernce 
will be interdisciplinary and will give participants ample opportunity to 
interact and to explore topics in depth.

Already confirmed speakers for the workshop include Rodney Cotterill, 
DTU; Hermann Haken, University of Stuttgart; Erik Fransen, Royal 
Institute of Technology, Stockholm; Galen Strawson, Jesus College, Oxford 
university; Thomas Metzinger, Germany; David Chalmers, Univ. of 
California, Santa Cruz; and Aaron Sloman, University of Birmingham.

Abstracts can be submitted for posters and concurrent session talks.  For 
further information, and abstract submission instructions, check out the 
UofA Center for COnsciousness Studies Homepage at:

<http://w3.arizona.edu/~uaextended/conferen/consc.html>

If you have any questions, feel free to contact me at <lis@axp.psl.ku.dk>.
